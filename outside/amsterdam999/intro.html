amsterdam999

July 10th, 1999. Amsterdam is a great city to visit. That morning I strolled along its famous canals lined with 17th century canal houses, I visited the Van Gogh Museum where there are over 200 paintings that drove me crazy, and finally simply sip a drink in one of Amsterdam's ubiquitous Brown Cafes while watching the world go by.
Before sunset I went to Vondelpark, the largest city park of Amsterdam. Palm readers and buskers, including African drummers, classical quartets, and jazz singers, provided a great experience of joy and peace. I could appreciate artistry, connect to the public, and just pass the time having a cigarette.
As darkness descends, the old city can look like an illustration from a fairytale, when thousands of little lights illuminate the bridges and historical buildings are floodlit. That's the facade I could not appreciate that much: jazz clubs and streets are a far better attraction to my heart and soul.
In fact Amsterdam is not only famous for its canals and buildings: its red-light district, liberal attitudes and trendy nightlife are amazing.
I think it is common to fall in love with this city, it's historic architecture and, most of all, with it's people. The Dutch and other tourists are here to have a good time and sharing that experience is where it's at! This is what connects this city to Prague, Rome or London: the essentially European hot humanity.
Another way to connect to mankind.

Just my personal vision.

Davide Gazzotti, "amsterdam999"
