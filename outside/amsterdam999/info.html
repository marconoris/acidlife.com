amsterdam999

Amsterdam stroke me in between a job trip, when I had a spare day while I was
waiting for my plane home.
It was the third time I was there, actually it was just another one day walkaround for the third time in my life.
I love that town. It does not shine in the magnificent lights of Paris, it does not recall the ragged beauties of London, and it does not share the ancient charm with Rome.
I love it the way I enjoyed Prague. It possess the gift of joining peace and art, emotions and joy. And, hard to believe I know, I did not smoke a single joint!
I portrayed the city shooting its people. I used my old full manual SLR on a couple of Kodak T400cn B/W films rated ad 250 ISO.
As I did not carry with me the winder, I found myself never taking unnecessary bracketed pictures of the same scene. I mostly used the 50mm lens, and only 4 of the 20 shots were taken with a 135mm. I had the film processed by a pretty standard photofinisher and I acquired the prints using my cheap off-brand A4 scanner. Some of the pictures you can see in this site would deserve a better electronic refinement, but, because of my chronicle lack of time, I cannot resist the temptation of showing them off today.
I should also point out that these photographs were merely intended as documentation, not art. Let us call it "reportage".
I just wanted to show the city as I could feel it in my hands (I always feel something touchy when I release the shutter).

Just my personal vision.

Davide Gazzotti, "amsterdam999"

 

Images and jotting by Davide Gazzotti, collected on July 10th, 1999.

Pictures shot with a Nikon FE and a 50mm lens, on Kodak T-MAX400cn film.
